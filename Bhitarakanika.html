BHITARKANIKA NATIONAL PARK

  • Bhitarkanika National Park covers an area of
    145 square kilometreand has been designated as a National Park since 1998..

  • The national park and wildlife sanctuary is inundated by a number of rivers -
    Brahmani, Baitarni, Dhamra, Pathsala and others.

  • The park is home to the endangered Saltwater Crocodile (Crocodylus porosus). White Crocodile, Indian python, King Cobra, black ibis, darters and many other species of flora and fauna.
