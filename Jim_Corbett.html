JIM CORBETT NATIONAL PARK

  • It is the oldest national park of India whichwas established in 1936 andcovers an area
    of 520.8 sq.km (201.1 sq. mi).

  • 110 tree species, 50 species of mammals, 580 bird species and
    25     reptile     species are found.

  • Some of the important species found are Barking deer, Himalayan Black Bears, Yellow-throated marten, Himalayan goral etc.
