NANDA DEVI NATIONAL PARK

  • It covers an area of 630.33 km2 (243.37 sq mi).

  • It was inscribed a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1988.

  • 312 floral species that include 17 rare species have been found here.

  • Fir, birch, rhododendron, and juniper are the main flora.

  • Vegetation is scarce In the inner sanctuary due to the dryness of the conditions.
    Ramani, alpine, prone mosses and lichens are other notable floral species.
