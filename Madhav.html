MADHAV NATIONAL PARK

  • It was originally the shooting reserve of the Maharaja who belonged to Gwalior and came into existence in
    1958 with the name, Shivpuri National Park..

  • The Madhav National Park is dispersed over a plot that measures 156 square kilometers.
    The park is unique have Sakhya and Madhav Sagar lakes in the park

  • Deep inside the Madhav National Park at its highest point, stands the exquisite George Castle at a height of almost 484 m

  • Some of the important species found are Nilgai, Chinkara and Chowsinga and Deer including Chital, Sambar and Barking Deer,Leopard, Wolf, Jackal, Fox.
